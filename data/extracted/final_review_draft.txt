ABSTRACT
This literature review examines key aspects of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its practical implications, drawing insights from two distinct perspectives. The first paper proposes taxonomies for Machine Learning and Deep Learning explainability, underscoring eXplainable AI (XAI) as vital for practical deployment and achieving Responsible AI—a methodology centered on fairness, explainability, and accountability. It identifies the inherent explainability barrier in modern sub-symbolic AI techniques. The second paper explores AI's multidisciplinary challenges and opportunities, highlighting its potential for societal improvement and economic development while cautioning against negative impacts on vulnerable populations. It emphasizes the need for effective human-AI interaction strategies, critical information quality, and AI's role in augmenting, rather than replacing, human work. While no strong common domain-specific themes emerged, one paper primarily focuses on methodological advancements for AI explainability, whereas the other addresses the broader societal results and safety considerations of AI implementation.

METHODS COMPARISON
The first paper, "Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI): Concepts, Taxonomies, Opportunities and Challenges toward Responsible AI," adopts a technical methodology, proposing and detailing taxonomies for the explainability of various Machine Learning models, including Deep Learning. Its problem framing centers on addressing the "barrier of explainability" inherent in modern sub-symbolic AI techniques, advocating XAI as a crucial feature for the practical deployment of AI and the development of Responsible Artificial Intelligence methodology, encompassing fairness, explainability, and accountability. In notable contrast, "Artificial Intelligence (AI): Multidisciplinary perspectives on emerging challenges, opportunities, and agenda for research, practice and policy" takes a broader, less technical approach focused on the societal and organizational implications of AI. This paper primarily examines the results and safety aspects of AI, framing the challenge as navigating the short-to-medium term transition of AI's onset, mitigating negative impacts on vulnerable societal aspects, and emphasizing strategies for human-AI interaction where AI augments rather than replaces human work. Thus, the former delves into the technical mechanisms for achieving model explainability, while the latter focuses on the broader strategic and ethical integration of AI into society and organizations.

RESULTS SYNTHESIS
The reviewed literature reveals a dual perspective on the practical implementation of Artificial Intelligence, highlighting both its technical hurdles and broader societal implications. "Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI): Concepts, Taxonomies, Opportunities and Challenges toward Responsible AI" primarily focuses on methodological advancements, proposing taxonomies for Machine Learning and Deep Learning explainability and identifying this as a crucial barrier for modern AI techniques toward achieving Responsible AI. In contrast, "Artificial Intelligence (AI): Multidisciplinary perspectives on emerging challenges, opportunities, and agenda for research, practice and policy" adopts a broader lens, detailing AI's potential to improve quality of life and economic development, alongside its capacity to negatively impact vulnerable societal aspects without effective human-AI interaction strategies. This second paper emphasizes AI's role in augmenting, rather than replacing, human work and underlines the critical need for high-quality information for effective change. Collectively, the papers demonstrate that successful AI deployment requires addressing both the inherent technical challenge of model explainability and the complex multidisciplinary considerations surrounding human-AI interaction, societal impact, and strategic organizational integration.
