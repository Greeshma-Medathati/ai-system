This review examines two distinct yet complementary facets of responsible AI development: AI harmlessness and explainable artificial intelligence (XAI). Constitutional AI research demonstrates that Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) significantly improves AI models' ability to follow instructions and achieve greater harmlessness, with specific probability clamping strategies further optimizing results. However, robustness against adversarial attacks remains a key challenge. Concurrently, XAI research highlights how integrating background knowledge enhances both explainability and performance. It introduces algorithms for bias reduction, exemplified in recidivism prediction, and underscores the intricate relationship between data fusion, privacy, and fairness. While Constitutional AI mainly focuses on results, method, and safety, XAI primarily centers on method, results, and evaluation. Despite lacking strong domain-specific common themes, these papers collectively illuminate the diverse methodological and evaluative considerations essential for ethical AI.
