This literature review examines key aspects of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its practical implications, drawing insights from two distinct perspectives. The first paper proposes taxonomies for Machine Learning and Deep Learning explainability, underscoring eXplainable AI (XAI) as vital for practical deployment and achieving Responsible AI—a methodology centered on fairness, explainability, and accountability. It identifies the inherent explainability barrier in modern sub-symbolic AI techniques. The second paper explores AI's multidisciplinary challenges and opportunities, highlighting its potential for societal improvement and economic development while cautioning against negative impacts on vulnerable populations. It emphasizes the need for effective human-AI interaction strategies, critical information quality, and AI's role in augmenting, rather than replacing, human work. While no strong common domain-specific themes emerged, one paper primarily focuses on methodological advancements for AI explainability, whereas the other addresses the broader societal results and safety considerations of AI implementation.
