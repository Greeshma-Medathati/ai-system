Common Themes:
*   Privacy in machine learning, particularly concerning the confidentiality or anonymity of training data, is a significant and complex area of research.
*   There is an inherent challenge in balancing privacy objectives with the desire for high model utility and computational efficiency.
*   Specific techniques and careful application are necessary to effectively address privacy issues in ML systems.

Major Differences:
*   **Focus of Privacy Concern:** Paper 1 focuses on achieving *formal data anonymization* using Differential Privacy (DP) as a proactive measure, whereas Paper 2 investigates *membership inference attacks* (a specific privacy threat) and empirical defensive strategies against them.
*   **Nature of Solutions:** Paper 1 advocates for Differential Privacy as a single, formal, and mathematically guaranteed privacy mechanism that requires architectural adjustments and precise accounting. Paper 2 explores multiple, distinct, and empirical defenses (e.g., Dropout, Model Stacking, statistical analysis) against a specific attack, without claiming formal guarantees.
*   **Scope of Privacy Guarantees:** Paper 1 emphasizes "formal data anonymization guarantees" for training data provided by DP. Paper 2 measures the effectiveness of defenses by their ability to "substantially reduc[e] attack performance," which is an empirical measure against a specific attack rather than a broad formal guarantee.

Overlapping Conclusions:
*   The privacy of training data is a critical concern for machine learning models, necessitating specific protective measures.
*   Effective privacy protection mechanisms in machine learning should aim to preserve or minimize negative impacts on model utility and accuracy.
