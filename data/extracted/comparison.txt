Common Themes:
*   Both papers address significant challenges and limitations associated with Large Language Models (LLMs).
*   Both discuss issues inherent in the current state-of-the-art LLM landscape, suggesting that existing approaches are insufficient in certain aspects.

Major Differences:
*   Paper 007e7d6cb64a7dfcdcad9fc6f4f0ba69fdc88203.pdf focuses on the practical deployment and operational efficiency of LLMs, specifically addressing issues like latency, resource consumption, bandwidth costs, and privacy concerns related to cloud-centric inference. Its proposed solution, EdgeShard, is an architectural framework for collaborative LLM inference using edge and cloud resources.
*   Paper 929305892d4ddae575a0fc23227a8139f7681632.pdf focuses on the security and safety vulnerabilities of LLMs, specifically their susceptibility to jailbreak attacks despite extensive safety training. It identifies underlying failure modes in safety training ("competing objectives" and "mismatched generalization") and proposes that these vulnerabilities are inherent to current safety methodologies.

Overlapping Conclusions:
*   Both papers conclude that current operational paradigms or underlying mechanisms for state-of-the-art LLMs face fundamental and unresolved issues. Paper 007e7d6cb64a7dfcdcad9fc6f4f0ba69fdc88203.pdf highlights the limitations of heavy cloud reliance for LLM inference regarding performance, cost, and privacy, while Paper 929305892d4ddae575a0fc23227a8139f7681632.pdf demonstrates that current safety training methods are fundamentally flawed, leading to inherent security vulnerabilities.
