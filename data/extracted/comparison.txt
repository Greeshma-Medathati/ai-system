Common Themes:
*   Both papers address significant challenges and limitations inherent in the current state of Large Language Models (LLMs).
*   Both highlight that existing or conventional approaches are insufficient to fully resolve these identified problems.

Major Differences:
*   **Focus Area:** Paper 007e7d6cb64a7dfcdcad9fc6f4f0ba69fdc88203.pdf focuses on the *computational, deployment, and resource management* challenges of LLMs (e.g., latency, cost, privacy, efficiency) using cloud and edge computing paradigms. In contrast, Paper 929305892d4ddae575a0fc23227a8139f7681632.pdf focuses on the *safety, security, and ethical vulnerabilities* of LLMs (e.g., jailbreak attacks, failures in safety training).
*   **Proposed Solutions:** Paper 007e7d6cb64a7dfcdcad9fc6f4f0ba69fdc88203.pdf proposes a specific architectural framework and algorithms (EdgeShard, collaborative edge computing) to optimize LLM inference. Paper 929305892d4ddae575a0fc23227a8139f7681632.pdf diagnoses the failure modes of current safety training and calls for "safety-capability parity" but does not propose a specific algorithmic or architectural solution for safety.

Overlapping Conclusions:
*   Both papers conclude that current state-of-the-art LLMs and their supporting systems/training methods have fundamental limitations that require novel approaches.
*   Both suggest that simply scaling existing methods (e.g., relying more on cloud for deployment, increasing current safety training data) will not resolve the core issues they identify.
*   Both advocate for more sophisticated and tailored solutions to overcome significant hurdles in making LLMs effective and reliable for widespread real-world application.
