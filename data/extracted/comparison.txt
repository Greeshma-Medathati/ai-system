*   Both papers focus on challenges and limitations inherent in Large Language Models (LLMs).

Major Differences:
*   **Area of Focus:** One paper primarily addresses the **operational efficiency, performance, and privacy** of LLM inference (latency, throughput, resource distribution), while the other focuses on **safety, security, and vulnerability** of LLMs to adversarial attacks (jailbreaks).

*   **Proposed Solutions:** One paper proposes a specific framework (EdgeShard) for collaborative LLM inference and an optimization algorithm, while the other identifies underlying failure modes and designs new attacks to demonstrate vulnerabilities, advocating for "safety-capability parity."

Overlapping Conclusions:
*   Current state-of-the-art LLMs, whether in deployment or internal safety mechanisms, possess significant limitations that require dedicated, innovative solutions.
*   Simply scaling current LLM technologies (e.g., larger models, more cloud resources, or more basic safety training) is insufficient to resolve fundamental underlying problems related to performance, privacy, or safety.
