The first paper, "Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI): Concepts, Taxonomies, Opportunities and Challenges toward Responsible AI," adopts a technical methodology, proposing and detailing taxonomies for the explainability of various Machine Learning models, including Deep Learning. Its problem framing centers on addressing the "barrier of explainability" inherent in modern sub-symbolic AI techniques, advocating XAI as a crucial feature for the practical deployment of AI and the development of Responsible Artificial Intelligence methodology, encompassing fairness, explainability, and accountability. In notable contrast, "Artificial Intelligence (AI): Multidisciplinary perspectives on emerging challenges, opportunities, and agenda for research, practice and policy" takes a broader, less technical approach focused on the societal and organizational implications of AI. This paper primarily examines the results and safety aspects of AI, framing the challenge as navigating the short-to-medium term transition of AI's onset, mitigating negative impacts on vulnerable societal aspects, and emphasizing strategies for human-AI interaction where AI augments rather than replaces human work. Thus, the former delves into the technical mechanisms for achieving model explainability, while the latter focuses on the broader strategic and ethical integration of AI into society and organizations.
