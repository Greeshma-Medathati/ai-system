The reviewed literature reveals a dual perspective on the practical implementation of Artificial Intelligence, highlighting both its technical hurdles and broader societal implications. "Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI): Concepts, Taxonomies, Opportunities and Challenges toward Responsible AI" primarily focuses on methodological advancements, proposing taxonomies for Machine Learning and Deep Learning explainability and identifying this as a crucial barrier for modern AI techniques toward achieving Responsible AI. In contrast, "Artificial Intelligence (AI): Multidisciplinary perspectives on emerging challenges, opportunities, and agenda for research, practice and policy" adopts a broader lens, detailing AI's potential to improve quality of life and economic development, alongside its capacity to negatively impact vulnerable societal aspects without effective human-AI interaction strategies. This second paper emphasizes AI's role in augmenting, rather than replacing, human work and underlines the critical need for high-quality information for effective change. Collectively, the papers demonstrate that successful AI deployment requires addressing both the inherent technical challenge of model explainability and the complex multidisciplinary considerations surrounding human-AI interaction, societal impact, and strategic organizational integration.
